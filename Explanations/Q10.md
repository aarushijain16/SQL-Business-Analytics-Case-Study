## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q10](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q10.sql). Identify pairs of products that are frequently purchased together in the same order.

## 🎯 Objective
To uncover product associations by identifying items that are commonly bought together, enabling better cross-selling and bundling strategies.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Performed a self-join on the order_items table to generate product pairs within the same order
- Used the condition `oi1.product_id < oi2.product_id` to avoid duplicate and reverse pair combinations
- Structured intermediate results using a Common Table Expression (CTE)
- Aggregated the frequency of each product pair using COUNT()

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
A self-join is required to compare products within the same order and generate combinations of items purchased together.  
The condition `product_id < product_id` ensures:
- No duplicate pairs (A-B and B-A)
- No self-pairing (A-A)  
COUNT() is used to measure how frequently each pair appears, which helps identify strong product associations.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Identify frequently bought together products for bundling  
- Improve cross-selling and recommendation strategies  
- Increase average order value (AOV)  
- Enhance customer experience through personalized suggestions
