## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q14](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q14.sql). Identify customers who are likely to churn by analyzing their purchase inactivity compared to their historical buying frequency.

## 🎯 Objective
To detect customers at risk of churn by comparing their current inactivity with their historical purchase behavior, enabling proactive retention strategies.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated customer-level metrics:
  - First order date using MIN()
  - Last order date using MAX()
  - Total number of orders using COUNT()
- Calculated average time between purchases using:
  (last_order - first_order) / (total_orders - 1)
- Derived current inactivity (recency) using:
  dataset’s latest order date - last_order
- Used CTEs to structure intermediate calculations
- Applied rule-based segmentation using CASE() to classify customers into New, Active, At Risk, and High Risk groups

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
The comparison between **current inactivity (current_gap)** and **historical purchase interval (avg_days_between_orders)** is key to identifying churn risk.  
MAX() OVER() is used to obtain the latest date in the dataset, ensuring recency is calculated correctly for historical data.  
NULLIF() prevents division by zero when customers have only one order.  
CASE() is used to segment customers based on their relative inactivity levels.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Identify customers who are at risk of churn before they stop purchasing  
- Prioritize high-risk customers for targeted retention campaigns  
- Personalize engagement strategies based on customer activity levels  
- Improve customer lifetime value by reducing churn
