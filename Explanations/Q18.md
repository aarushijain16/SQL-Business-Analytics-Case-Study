## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q18](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q18.sql). Calculate the percentage of customers who have made more than one purchase to evaluate customer retention and loyalty.

## 🎯 Objective
To measure customer retention by identifying repeat customers and understanding the proportion of customers making multiple purchases.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated total number of orders per customer using COUNT()
- Used a Common Table Expression (CTE) to structure customer-level data
- Calculated:
  - Total customers
  - Repeat customers (orders ≥ 2)
  - One-time customers (orders = 1)
- Derived repeat customer percentage using aggregated values

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
COUNT() is used to measure purchase frequency at the customer level.  
The FILTER clause is used for conditional aggregation, allowing clean and efficient counting of repeat and one-time customers within the same query.  
This approach avoids multiple CASE statements and improves readability.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Measure customer retention and loyalty  
- Identify reliance on repeat vs one-time customers  
- Evaluate long-term customer value potential  
- Design strategies to convert one-time buyers into repeat customers
