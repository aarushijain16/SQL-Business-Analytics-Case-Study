## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q11](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q11.sql). Calculate Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) by estimating the total revenue generated per customer along with their average order value and purchase frequency.

## 🎯 Objective
To evaluate customer value by analyzing their total contribution, purchasing frequency, and average spend, enabling better customer segmentation and prioritization.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated customer-level metrics:
  - Total number of orders using COUNT()
  - Total revenue using SUM()
- Calculated average order value (AOV) by dividing total revenue by total orders
- Used a Common Table Expression (CTE) to structure intermediate results
- Ranked customers based on total revenue using ROW_NUMBER()
- Applied rule-based segmentation using CASE() to classify customers into value segments

## ⚙️ Why This Function?
ROW_NUMBER() is used to assign a unique rank to each customer based on total revenue, ensuring a clear ordering of customers by value.  
CASE() is used to segment customers into high, medium, and low-value groups based on predefined revenue thresholds.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Identify high-value customers for targeted retention and personalized strategies  
- Understand customer spending patterns and purchasing behavior  
- Allocate marketing and retention efforts more effectively  
- Prioritize customers based on their revenue contribution
