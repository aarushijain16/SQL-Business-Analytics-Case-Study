## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q15](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q15.sql). Identify high-performing, underperforming, and risky products by combining sales performance and inventory movement.

## 🎯 Objective
To evaluate product performance based on revenue contribution and sales velocity, enabling better inventory planning and product strategy.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated product-level metrics:
  - Total revenue using SUM()
  - Total units sold using SUM()
  - Selling duration using MAX(order_date) - MIN(order_date)
- Calculated average daily sales (sales velocity) as total_units / selling_days
- Used CTEs to structure intermediate results
- Computed benchmark values:
  - Average revenue across products
  - Average sales velocity across products
- Compared each product against benchmarks
- Segmented products into Star, Cash Cow, Growth, and Risky categories using CASE()

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
Benchmarking is used to evaluate product performance relative to overall business performance rather than in isolation.  
Average revenue and average sales velocity act as thresholds to classify products into meaningful segments.  
NULLIF() is used to avoid division by zero when calculating sales velocity.  
CASE() is used to categorize products based on their relative performance.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Identify high-performing products (Stars) to prioritize for scaling and promotion  
- Detect fast-moving but low-revenue products (Growth) with potential upside  
- Recognize high-revenue but slow-moving products (Cash Cows) that may require inventory optimization  
- Flag risky or underperforming products for clearance, discounting, or discontinuation
