## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q16](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q16.sql). Identify each customer’s most frequently purchased product category to enable personalized recommendations.

## 🎯 Objective
To determine customer preferences by identifying their most frequently purchased category, enabling targeted recommendations and improved customer experience.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Joined orders, order_items, and products tables to capture customer purchase behavior
- Aggregated total quantity purchased per category at the customer level using SUM()
- Used a Common Table Expression (CTE) to structure intermediate results
- Applied ROW_NUMBER() partitioned by customer_id to rank categories based on purchase count
- Selected the top-ranked category (rank = 1) for each customer

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
SUM(quantity) is used to measure customer preference based on purchase volume.  
ROW_NUMBER() is used to assign a unique rank to each category within a customer group, ensuring only one top category is selected per customer.  
Partitioning by customer_id allows independent ranking for each customer.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Deliver personalized product recommendations  
- Improve cross-selling within preferred categories  
- Enhance customer engagement and shopping experience  
- Increase average order value through targeted suggestions
