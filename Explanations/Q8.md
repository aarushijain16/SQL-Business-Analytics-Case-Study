## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q8](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q8.sql). Identify what percentage of total revenue is contributed by top customers and evaluate whether the business follows the Pareto (80/20) principle.

## 🎯 Objective
To determine whether a small percentage of customers contribute a majority of the revenue, helping assess customer dependency and revenue concentration.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated total revenue at the customer level using SUM()
- Ordered customers by total revenue in descending order
- Used SUM() OVER() to calculate cumulative revenue across customers
- Calculated total revenue across all customers using a window function
- Derived cumulative revenue percentage for each customer
- Segmented customers based on whether they fall within the top 80% revenue contribution

## ⚙️ Why This Function?
SUM() OVER() is used to compute cumulative revenue while retaining individual customer-level rows.  
Unlike GROUP BY, it allows calculation of running totals across ordered data, which is essential for Pareto analysis.  
CASE is used to segment customers based on their cumulative revenue contribution into high-value (top 80%) and remaining customers.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Identify dependency on a small group of high-value customers  
- Evaluate revenue concentration and associated business risk  
- Design retention strategies for top customers  
- Develop growth strategies for lower-contributing customers to balance revenue distribution
