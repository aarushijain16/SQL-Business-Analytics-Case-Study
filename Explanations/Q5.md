## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q5](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q5.sql). Analyze customer retention using cohort analysis by tracking how many customers return in subsequent months after their first purchase.

## 🎯 Objective
To understand customer retention patterns by grouping customers based on their first purchase month and analyzing their activity over time.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Defined cohort as the month of a customer’s first order using MIN(order_date)
- Used DATE_TRUNC() to group data at the monthly level
- Joined cohort data with order history to track customer activity across months
- Counted distinct customers for each cohort-month combination to measure retention

## ⚙️ Why This Function?
DATE_TRUNC() is used to group dates at the month level while preserving both month and year, making it suitable for time-based analysis.  
EXTRACT() was avoided because it returns only the month number without year context, which can lead to incorrect grouping across years.  
COUNT(DISTINCT) ensures each customer is counted only once per month, avoiding duplication.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Understand customer retention and repeat purchase behavior  
- Identify how long customers stay engaged after their first purchase  
- Detect drop-off points where customers stop returning  
- Improve retention strategies and customer lifecycle management
