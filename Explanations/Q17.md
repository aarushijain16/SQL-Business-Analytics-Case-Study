## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q17](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q17.sql). Analyze how different price ranges impact sales volume and revenue to understand price sensitivity and optimize pricing strategy.

## 🎯 Objective
To evaluate how different price bands influence customer demand and revenue contribution, enabling better pricing decisions.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Categorized products into price bands (Low, Medium, High) using CASE()
- Aggregated total units sold and total revenue for each category and price band
- Calculated average selling price as total_revenue / total_units
- Used window functions to compute:
  - Volume share (%) of each price band
  - Revenue share (%) of each price band
- Compared performance across price bands within each category

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
CASE() is used to group products into meaningful price segments for analysis.  
SUM() aggregates sales and revenue to measure performance.  
Window functions (SUM() OVER()) are used to calculate relative contribution (percentage share) without collapsing rows, enabling comparison across price bands.  
NULLIF() prevents division by zero when calculating averages.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Identify price sensitivity by comparing volume vs revenue contribution  
- Detect whether lower-priced products drive volume while higher-priced products drive revenue  
- Optimize pricing strategy by balancing demand and profitability  
- Adjust product positioning and discounts based on price band performance
