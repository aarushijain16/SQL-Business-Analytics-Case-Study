## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q7](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q7.sql). Identify repeat customers and calculate the average time between their orders.

## 🎯 Objective
To analyze customer purchase frequency by measuring the average time gap between consecutive orders for repeat customers.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated total number of orders at the customer level using COUNT()
- Identified first and last order dates using MIN() and MAX()
- Calculated total time span between first and last purchase
- Divided the time span by (total_orders - 1) to estimate the average gap between consecutive orders
- Filtered only repeat customers (customers with more than one order)

## ⚙️ Why This Function?
The formula `(last_order - first_order) / (total_orders - 1)` is used to estimate the average time between consecutive purchases.  
`NULLIF()` is used to prevent division by zero when total_orders = 1.  
Filtering with `WHERE total_orders > 1` ensures only repeat customers are included in the analysis.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Understand how frequently customers make repeat purchases  
- Identify highly engaged vs low-frequency customers  
- Optimize timing for follow-ups, promotions, and re-engagement campaigns  
- Improve customer retention by targeting customers based on their purchase cycle
