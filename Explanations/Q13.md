## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q13](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q13.sql). Forecast next period demand for each product using historical sales trends.

## 🎯 Objective
To estimate future product demand based on recent sales trends, enabling better inventory planning and production decisions.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated monthly product-level sales using SUM(quantity)
- Used DATE_TRUNC() to group sales at the monthly level
- Applied LAG() to retrieve previous month’s sales for each product
- Calculated trend as the difference between current and previous month sales
- Forecasted next month’s demand using the formula: current sales + trend
- Classified products as Growing, Declining, or Stable based on trend

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
LAG() is used to access the previous month’s sales within the same product group, enabling trend calculation.  
COALESCE() is used to handle NULL values for the first month where no previous data exists.  
The forecasting formula `(current + (current - previous))` is a simple trend-based approach that projects future demand based on recent momentum.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Anticipate future demand and plan inventory accordingly  
- Avoid stockouts for growing products and overstock for declining ones  
- Align production with demand trends  
- Make data-driven operational and investment decisions
