## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q12](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q12.sql). Identify stockout risk and overstock situations by estimating how long current inventory can sustain based on average daily sales.

## 🎯 Objective
To evaluate inventory health by determining whether products are understocked, optimally stocked, or overstocked, enabling better inventory planning and control.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated total units sold at the product level using SUM()
- Calculated selling duration using MAX(order_date) and MIN(order_date)
- Derived average daily sales using total_units_sold / selling_days
- Simulated current stock using a demand-based stocking strategy (since actual stock data is unavailable)
- Calculated days_left as current_stock / avg_daily_sales
- Classified inventory status into Stockout Risk, Healthy, and Overstock using CASE()

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
Since actual inventory data is not available, current stock is estimated using a demand-based proxy to reflect realistic stocking behavior.  
The key metric is **days_left**, which indicates how long current inventory will last based on sales velocity.  
This metric helps determine when to reorder and whether inventory levels are aligned with demand.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Prevent stockouts and avoid lost sales opportunities  
- Reduce excess inventory and holding costs  
- Improve cash flow by avoiding over-investment in stock  
- Align inventory levels with product demand for efficient operations
