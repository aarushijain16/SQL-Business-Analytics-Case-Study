## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q20](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q20.sql). Analyze revenue trends over time and measure volatility to understand business stability and predictability.

## 🎯 Objective
To evaluate the consistency of revenue over time and assess how stable or volatile the business performance is.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated monthly revenue using SUM() and DATE_TRUNC()
- Used a Common Table Expression (CTE) to structure time-series data
- Calculated:
  - Average monthly revenue using AVG()
  - Revenue variability using STDDEV()
- Derived volatility ratio as (standard deviation / average revenue)
- Classified revenue stability into Stable, Moderate, and Highly Volatile categories using CASE()

## ⚙️ Why This Approach?
STDDEV() is used to measure how much revenue fluctuates over time.  
The ratio (STDDEV / AVG) represents the **coefficient of variation**, which standardizes volatility relative to average performance.  
This makes it easier to compare stability regardless of revenue scale.  
NULLIF() prevents division by zero errors.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Assess revenue predictability and financial stability  
- Identify periods of high fluctuation and potential risk  
- Improve forecasting and budgeting decisions  
- Plan operations more effectively based on revenue consistency
