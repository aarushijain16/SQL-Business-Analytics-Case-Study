## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q4](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q4.sql). Identify repeat customers (customers who placed more than one order) and calculate the percentage of repeat customers.

## 🎯 Objective
To measure customer retention by identifying customers who make multiple purchases and evaluating the proportion of repeat customers.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Aggregated total number of orders at the customer level using COUNT()
- Used HAVING clause to filter customers with more than one order (repeat customers)
- Structured intermediate results using a Common Table Expression (CTE)
- Calculated repeat customer percentage using conditional aggregation with CASE()

## ⚙️ Why This Function?
CASE() is used for conditional aggregation to count only those customers who have placed more than one order.  
It allows selective counting within an aggregate function, making it useful for calculating metrics like repeat customer percentage.  
Alternatively, this can also be achieved using FILTER in PostgreSQL for cleaner syntax.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Measure customer retention and loyalty  
- Identify dependency on repeat vs new customers  
- Design strategies to convert one-time buyers into repeat customers  
- Strengthen engagement with existing loyal customers
