## 🧠 Problem Statement
[Q6](https://github.com/aarushijain16/SQL-Business-Analytics-Case-Study/blob/main/Queries/Q6.sql). Calculate monthly cohort retention percentage by tracking how many customers return in subsequent months after their first purchase.

## 🎯 Objective
To measure customer retention rates over time by analyzing the percentage of customers from each cohort who remain active in later months.

## 🛠️ Approach
- Defined cohort as the month of a customer’s first order using MIN(order_date)
- Used DATE_TRUNC() to group data at the monthly level
- Joined cohort data with order history to track customer activity across months
- Counted distinct active customers for each cohort-month combination
- Calculated cohort size (total customers in each cohort)
- Derived retention percentage by dividing active customers by cohort size

## ⚙️ Why This Function?
DATE_TRUNC() is used to group dates at the month level while preserving both month and year, ensuring accurate time-based analysis.  
EXTRACT() was avoided because it only returns the month number without year context.  
COUNT(DISTINCT) ensures each customer is counted only once per month, preventing duplication.  
The retention percentage is calculated by dividing active customers by cohort size to normalize retention across different cohort sizes.

## 💡 Business Insight
This analysis helps businesses:
- Measure customer retention rates over time  
- Compare performance across different customer cohorts  
- Identify strong vs weak cohorts based on retention behavior  
- Detect drop-off patterns and improve customer lifecycle strategies
