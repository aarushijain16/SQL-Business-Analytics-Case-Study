
# 📊 SQL Business Analytics Case Study: From Data to Decisions

## 🧠 Overview

This project demonstrates how SQL can be used to solve real-world business problems across customer analytics, revenue optimization, inventory planning, and demand forecasting.

The goal of this case study is not just to write SQL queries, but to translate data into actionable business insights that support decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze customer behavior and retention patterns
* Identify top-performing products and revenue drivers
* Evaluate pricing strategies and sales performance
* Detect churn risk and improve customer lifecycle management
* Forecast demand trends for better planning
* Optimize inventory and product strategy

---

## 🧩 Key Business Problems Solved

* Which products and price ranges generate the highest revenue?
* Who are the most valuable customers?
* What is the repeat purchase rate and customer loyalty level?
* Which customers are at risk of churn?
* How stable is the business revenue over time?
* How should inventory and production be planned?

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Techniques

* **SQL (PostgreSQL)**
* Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
* Window Functions (ROW_NUMBER, LAG, etc.)
* Aggregations and Grouping
* Cohort Analysis
* RFM Segmentation
* Trend-Based Forecasting

## 📊 Key Analyses Performed

### 1. Revenue & Product Analytics

* Top-performing products
* Revenue contribution by price bands
* Product performance segmentation (Star, Cash Cow, etc.)

### 2. Customer Analytics

* Top customers by revenue
* Repeat purchase rate
* RFM segmentation
* Customer churn risk analysis

### 3. Sales & Order Insights

* Cumulative revenue trends
* Basket size and order value analysis
* Market basket (product affinity) analysis

### 4. Inventory & Operations

* Stock coverage and inventory risk
* Sales velocity analysis
* Product-level demand patterns

### 5. Forecasting & Trends

* Monthly demand forecasting using SQL
* Trend classification (Growing / Declining / Stable)
* Revenue volatility and stability analysis

---

## 💡 Key Insights

* A small percentage of customers contribute a large share of revenue (Pareto principle)
* Repeat customers play a critical role in long-term business sustainability
* Certain price bands drive higher volume, indicating price sensitivity
* Inventory planning should be aligned with product velocity to avoid stockouts and overstock
* Revenue volatility directly impacts operational and financial planning
